Sai Swathi Thota acts in a play

Name of the play:Ghosts

Author: Henrik Ibsen

REGINA

[In a low voice.]

What do you want? Stop where you are. You're positively dripping.

ENGSTRAND

It's the Lord's own rain, my girl.

REGINA

[Mutters.]

Pleasant journey to you!

ENGSTRAND

[With a twist of his shoulders.]

Oh, of course! I'm to have the blame for everything.

MANDERS

It is just in.

[Enters the sitting-room.]

Terrible weather we have been having lately.

REGINA

[Follows him.]

It's such blessed weather for the country, sir.

MRS. ALVING

[Holds out her hand.]

Welcome, my dear Pastor.

MANDERS

[Quickly.]

I left it down at the inn. I shall sleep there to-night.

MRS. ALVING

Certainly.

[She sits by the table.]

OSWALD

Oh, I beg your pardon; I thought you were in the study.

[Comes forward.]

Good-morning, Pastor Manders.

MANDERS

[Staring.]

Ah--! How strange--!

REGINA

[Turns away; half aloud.]

Ugh--! And that leg too!
